Cookie Man

Once upon a time, in a land near and far, There was a man, walking down the street, like a star. He was talking and humming, a happy tune, But then, all of a sudden, his body and head turned into a giant chocolate chip cookie, like a big balloon.

He looked down at himself and began to shout, "Oh my goodness, what is this all about?" He looked like a cookie, big and round, He couldn't believe what had just astounded.

He tried to walk but stumbled and fell, He was a cookie, how could he tell? He tried to talk, but all that came out, Were crumbs and chocolate chips, without a doubt.

He was confused, and quite scared, He didn't know how to get himself repaired. He walked down the street, as cookies do, But people were staring, they hadn't a clue.

He tried to explain, but they couldn't understand, All they saw was a cookie, in the shape of a man. He was sad and alone, in his cookie form, He missed his old body, and wished to be reborn.

But then, a kind witch, took pity on him, She said, "I'll help you, don't be grim." With a wave of her wand, and a magical spell, He was back to his old self, and all was well.

He thanked the witch, and went on his way, But he never forgot, his cookie day. He learned a valuable lesson, that's for sure, Be careful what you wish for, or you may turn into something obscure.
